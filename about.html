Who Am I?

Hello, I'm Taegyeong Kim. TG or TK for short. I'm a junior web-developer who lives in Busan, Korea.

How Did I make This?

I made the website with HTML5 and CSS3. My first thought was my portfolio should look stylish. So I searched for hours and found some CSS and Jquery animations I could apply to the website.

I edited them little bit, and put them. Yet that didn't satisfy me. It didn't look like "my" porfolio.

I shouldn't have used someone else's works. I deleted all of the codes I got from others, and started to edit the website with my own codes.

Why Did I Choose Coding for Profession?

I was certain that I would become a classical music professor until I failed to enter a high school of art.

Then I decided to go to Busan University of Foreign Studies and major in English that I was good at besides playing the piano.

After I graduated, I kept searching on the Internet since I didn't know what to do.

One day, I came across web design. I got some information about it, and I enrolled for an institute that teaches technologies to get a job. I received some money from the government while taking lessons on web design.

First thing I learned was how to use Adobe Illustrator. However, I didn't like design classes.

I started to learn HTML and CSS myself on W3Schools. When teacher started to teach coding, I already finished HTML and moved on to CSS. That was the time I began to like coding.

Soon I made a decision to become a front-end developer.

How Did I Learn to Code?

As I mentioned before, I learned to code at the web design class of the institute for the first time. Teacher wasn't helpful because her lessons were heavily design-based.

I went to W3Schools to learn HTML and CSS. After some months, I was able to create a website with a navigation bar and semantic tags.

Then I moved on to Codecademy to learn JavaScript. I made it to lesson 4: Scope, but Codecademy didn't give adequate information on each lesson. I moved on to FreeCodeCamp instead.

Unlike Codecademy, FreeCodeCamp provided every lesson for free. With detailed explanations, I was able to understand basic concepts of JavaScript.

Still, FreeCodeCamp didn't have an app to study all the time, and their mobile page wasn't suited to exercises. I found SoloLearn.

SoloLearn had great community feature to get help from other learners. When the lesson had inaccuracies or lacked some details, I looked comment section to see corrections from users. And the app was free. Now I'm at the finish line of basics.

Why I like to Code?

I like that coding has concrete formulas that have already assigned answers, but we could make various things freely by using it.
